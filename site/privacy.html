Privacy

The short version: this site counts its readers with software we run ourselves. Nothing about you is sold, shared, or sent to an ad network, because none of those things exist here.

What we measure

musterr.dev uses Matomo, an open-source analytics tool, self-hosted on our own server. For each visit it records:

  • the pages you view and the site that referred you here,
  • your browser and device type,
  • your approximate location (city level), derived from an anonymized IP address. The last two octets are discarded before anything is stored, so 203.0.113.7 is kept only as 203.0.0.0. We cannot identify you from it.

Cookies

Matomo sets a first-party cookie (_pk_id, plus a short-lived session cookie) so that a returning browser is counted once instead of twice. It works only on this domain, is never used for advertising, and is not shared with anyone. There is no third-party ad tech, no fingerprinting service, and no social widget on this site.

What we honor and discard

  • We respect both browser privacy signals. If your browser sends Global Privacy Control (Sec-GPC), the analytics script never runs. If it sends the older Do Not Track signal, the server discards the visit. Either way, you are not tracked at all.
  • Raw visit logs are deleted after 13 months; only aggregate statistics (counts, not visits) are kept beyond that.
  • All analytics data stays on infrastructure we operate. No third party receives it.

The demo portal

If you log into the read-only demo (demo@muster.dev), the app stores a session token in your browser. That token exists solely so the login works; it is not used for tracking.
